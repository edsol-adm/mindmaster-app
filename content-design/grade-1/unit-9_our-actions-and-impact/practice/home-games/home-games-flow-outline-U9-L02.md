## Home Game 2A ‚Äì Ripple Builder: Watch It Grow

> Jump to: [Setup](#setup) ¬∑ [Play](#play) ¬∑ [Scoring](#scoring) ¬∑ [Win Condition](#win-condition) ¬∑ [Continuity Note](#continuity-note)

### Learning Objective

Students understand that repeated small actions accumulate to create larger patterns and outcomes, and recognize that even small everyday choices carry real weight in shaping what happens for themselves, others, and the environment.

### Game File Name & Format

`hg-09.02-01.html` ‚Äî Simulation / Sandbox with Time Progression (Science App format)

A visual simulation game where players perform small repeated actions (water plant, say something kind, tidy one thing) over many ‚Äúdays‚Äù (fast-forwarded) to see cumulative effects. Players experiment with different patterns (frequency, consistency) and observe long-term patterns emerge.

### Game Purpose

This game demonstrates accumulation mechanics by letting players see small actions compound into significant effects. Unlike one-time cause-effect games, this emphasizes repetition over time‚Äîthe core insight of Lesson 2. Players experiment with different patterns to discover how consistency shapes outcomes.

### Estimated Play Time

9‚Äì12 minutes
Each scenario: 3‚Äì4 minutes (30 days √ó 3‚Äì5 seconds per day)
Final Comparison Lab: 2 minutes
Total if playing all three scenarios: ~12 min

### Setup

Opening Screen:

* Title: "Ripple Builder: Watch It Grow"
* Visual: Sam the Scientist (recurring avatar) standing beside three empty "growth zones" (plant pot, friendship meter, clean room display)
* Instruction: "Small actions add up! Pick a goal, do small actions every day, and watch what grows!"

### Play

Core Mechanic: Daily Action Selection + Time-Lapse Visualization

SCENARIO 1: Grow a Garden

Setup:

* Scene: Garden plot with small seed planted in soil
* Calendar appears: Day 1
* Instruction: "This seed needs care. Will you water it today?"
* Two buttons: "Water the plant" | "Skip today"

Daily Cycle (30 days compressed):
Each "day" lasts 3‚Äì5 seconds of real time. Player chooses action for that day.

If player chooses "Water the plant" consistently:

* Day 1: Seed in soil (no visible change yet)
* Day 3: Tiny sprout appears
* Day 7: Small stem with leaves
* Day 14: Plant grows taller, more leaves
* Day 21: Buds appear
* Day 30: Full bloom‚Äîbeautiful flower!

Visual feedback each day:

* Water animation plays (watering can pouring)
* Plant growth slightly visible
* Progress bar: "Growth: X%"

If player skips days:

* Plant stops growing
* After 3 consecutive skipped days: plant droops slightly
* If player resumes watering: plant recovers and continues growing (demonstrates that restarting helps!)

Day Counter visible: "Day X of 30"

At Day 30 (End of Garden Scenario):

* Final result shows based on consistency:

  * Watered 25‚Äì30 days: Full blooming garden, vibrant colors
  * Watered 15‚Äì24 days: Healthy plant, some flowers
  * Watered 10‚Äì14 days: Small plant, struggling
  * Watered <10 days: Wilted plant
* Sam appears: "Look at what happened! Every time you watered the plant‚Äîjust a small action‚Äîit added up. Day by day, the effects grew. That's how small actions work!"

Comparison Screen:

* Shows side-by-side visual: "If you water every day vs. if you skip many days"
* Dramatic difference illustrated

## Home Game 2B ‚Äì Chain Reaction Challenge

### Learning Objective

Students provide examples of how small daily choices build into bigger effects, compare cumulative effects of helpful versus unhelpful actions, and apply understanding that their everyday choices carry real weight.

### Game File Name & Format

`hg-09.02-02.html` ‚Äî Physics Puzzle / Rube Goldberg Machine Builder (Puzzle Game format)

A creative construction game where players build chain reactions by placing small action tiles that trigger sequential effects. Each small action connects to the next, demonstrating how tiny choices link together to create large outcomes.

### Game Purpose

This game makes accumulation tangible through spatial, visual problem-solving. Players see small actions literally chain together, reinforcing that one small choice leads to another, which leads to another‚Äîcreating powerful cumulative effects. The physics-puzzle format engages spatial reasoning while teaching the lesson concept.

---

### Setup

Opening Screen:

* Title: "Chain Reaction Challenge"
* Visual: Maya (recurring avatar) standing beside a Rube Goldberg-style contraption with missing pieces

First Puzzle Screen:

* Background: Simple workshop scene
* Left side: "Action Tiles Bank" showing available small actions (draggable icons)

### Play

* Small cup (catches water)

## How Lesson 2 Fits into the Full Learning Arc

Lesson 2 deepens Unit 9 by introducing the principle of **accumulation**‚Äîsmall, repeated actions that compound into larger outcomes over time. Through both home games, students move from *noticing* single effects to *tracking* how consistency and sequence amplify results. This builds self‚Äëefficacy: if repeated small choices change what happens, then I can shape my day, my relationships, and my environment.

* **Lesson 2 (this document):** Demonstrates that repetition and sequence matter. Learners test how daily actions add up across contexts (garden, friendship, tidy room) and build functioning chains‚Äîturning ‚Äúlittle choices‚Äù into visible change.
* **Lesson 1:** Established basic cause‚Äëand‚Äëeffect awareness‚Äîthe first ripple of agency that sets something in motion and prepares students to examine patterns over time.
* **Lesson 3:** Advances from understanding to *intentionality*: students predict consequences and choose helpful actions on purpose, selecting sequences that lead toward desired effects.
* **Lesson 4:** Completes the cycle by treating results as feedback; patterns can be redirected and new helpful routines can replace unhelpful ones.

> üå± **Learning Arc Summary:** Noticing cause and effect ‚Üí understanding accumulation ‚Üí choosing actions intentionally ‚Üí learning from feedback.
> By the end of Unit 9, students internalise that everyday decisions‚Äîespecially small ones repeated‚Äîcarry real weight. This shifts responsibility from rule‚Äëfollowing to informed, confident agency, laying the groundwork for Unit 10‚Äôs teamwork focus where reliable contributions compound into collective impact.
