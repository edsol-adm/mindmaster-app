### Game Purpose

### Setup

### Play
**Core Gameplay:**  

**Building Phase (8 rounds):**

- Shape: Square, rectangle, triangle, L-shape (representing different "roles")

**Round Structure:**

  - Physics: Complex balancing required

## How Lesson 1 Fits into the Full Learning Arc

Lesson 1 introduces **the family as a childâ€™s first team**, marking the shift from understanding individual psychological processes to exploring social systems. The two home games transform this idea into embodied learning: *Family Role Relay* illustrates that families thrive when responsibilities are shared rather than carried alone, while *Teamwork Tower Builder* deepens that understanding through balance and structure, showing that stability arises from cooperation among diverse roles.

- **Lesson 1 (this document):** Establishes family as the foundational social system where cooperation, care, and role-distribution are first learned.  
- **Lesson 2:** Extends these teamwork principles into friendships, where children apply cooperation skills outside the family to peers.  
- **Lesson 3:** Introduces conflict as a normal part of relationships and teaches cooperative problem-solving as the mechanism that keeps teams strong.  
- **Lesson 4:** Broadens perspective toward inclusion and empathy, emphasizing that families and friendships come in many forms, and strength lies in care and respect, not uniformity.
