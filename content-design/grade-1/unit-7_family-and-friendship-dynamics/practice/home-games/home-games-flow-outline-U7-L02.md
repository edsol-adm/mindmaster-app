## Home Game 2A ‚Äì Friendship Island Explorer

### Learning Objective
Students will identify the key qualities that make friendships strong (kindness, listening, trust, sharing, taking turns, respect) and understand that being a good friend is a learnable skill that improves with practice.

### Game File Name & Format
`hg-07.02-01.html` ‚Äî Exploration/Discovery (Adventure Game - navigate environment, unlock areas)

### Game Purpose
This game teaches that friendship skills are discoverable, learnable abilities that can be practiced and mastered. By exploring distinct island regions (each representing a friendship skill), children learn to identify and differentiate the six core friendship qualities through active discovery rather than passive instruction.

### Estimated Play Time
8‚Äì12 minutes (6 regions, approximately 1‚Äì2 minutes per region to find 3 gems; longer if seeking all sparkles)

## How Lesson 2 Fits into the Full Learning Arc

Lesson 2 extends the shift from individual understanding to social systems by moving from **family teamwork** to **friendship as a learnable skill**. The two home games translate this into practice: *Friendship Island Explorer* helps students **identify and name** the six core friendship skills through discovery, while *Kindness Chain Reaction* shows how **specific actions** reliably produce **positive social effects**. Together, they reinforce that friendship competence grows through observation, practice, and feedback‚Äînot through personality or luck.

- **Lesson 1:** Establishes family as the foundational social system where cooperation, role-distribution, and mutual support are first learned.  
- **Lesson 2 (this document):** Frames friendship as a **skill set**‚Äîkindness, listening, sharing, taking turns, trust, and respect‚Äîthat can be **observed, practiced, and improved** through deliberate actions.  
- **Lesson 3:** Normalizes conflict as part of relationships and teaches **cooperative problem-solving** to repair and strengthen friendships when challenges arise.  
- **Lesson 4:** Broadens perspective to **inclusion and empathy**, emphasizing that families and friendships come in many forms, and that **care and respect**‚Äînot uniformity‚Äîmake relationships strong.

> üß† **Learning Arc Summary:** Family as Team ‚Üí Friendship as Skill ‚Üí Problem-Solving as Cooperation ‚Üí Diversity as Strength.  
> By progressing from structural understanding (family teams) to actionable skills (friendship behaviors), students build **self-efficacy** (‚ÄúI can do friendship well‚Äù) that prepares them to handle conflict constructively and to value diversity with empathy‚Äîkey steps toward social **resilience**.
