## Home Game 4A ‚Äì Expression Around the World
> Jump to: [Setup](#setup) ¬∑ [Play](#play) ¬∑ [Scoring](#scoring) ¬∑ [Win Condition](#win-condition) ¬∑ [Continuity Note](#continuity-note)

### Learning Objective
Students will recognize that all people experience feelings, distinguish between internal feelings (universal) and external expressions (varied), and identify different ways the same feeling might be expressed by different people or cultures.

### Game File Name & Format
`hg-06.04-01.html` ‚Äî Interactive Fiction (from Generic: Adventure Games)

### Game Purpose
Children read and navigate through branching story scenarios featuring characters from different cultures and families, discovering that while everyone experiences feelings (universal), the way feelings are expressed varies (diverse). Choices reveal different expressions and teach respectful curiosity.

### Estimated Play Time
9‚Äì10 minutes

---

### Setup
**Opening Screen:**  
"You're about to meet friends from around the world! They all have feelings‚Äîbut they might show them in different ways."  

**World Map:**  
Simplified, colorful map showing 5 locations:  
- Home (child's neighborhood)  
- Japan (school setting)  
- Mexico (family celebration)  
- Kenya (community gathering)  
- Norway (quiet home)  

**Character:**  
Player avatar (simple, gender-neutral) appears: "I want to learn how different people show feelings!"  

**Feeling Guide Book:**  
Small book icon in corner tracking discoveries  

**Visual Style:**  
Illustrated storybook aesthetic, respectful cultural representations, warm colors

### Play

#### Phase 1: Home Story (Minutes 1‚Äì2)

**Step 1: Familiar Setting**  
Story text: "You're at recess. Your friend Maya just won the relay race."  
Illustration shows Maya jumping and cheering loudly  
Question appears: "How is Maya showing her happy feeling?"  
Child observes and clicks "Next"  

**Step 2: Different Expression**  
Story continues: "Your other friend, Ben, also won a prize in art class."  
Illustration shows Ben sitting quietly, small smile, looking at his artwork  
Question: "Is Ben happy too?"  
Two choices:  
- "No, Ben doesn't look happy‚Äîhe's too quiet" ‚ùå  
- "Yes, Ben might be happy too‚Äîjust showing it differently" ‚úì  

**Step 3: Teaching Moment**  
If correct choice: Story continues with Ben's thought bubble: "I feel so happy! I like to enjoy my happy feelings quietly."  
Text: "You discovered: Maya shows happy with energy and noise. Ben shows happy with quiet smiling. Same feeling, different expression!"  
Feeling Guide Book updates: "Discovery 1: Same feeling, many ways to show it"  

---

#### Phase 2: Japan Story (Minutes 3‚Äì4)

**Step 4: New Cultural Context**  
Map zooms to Japan  
Story: "You visit a school in Japan. Everyone is taking a test. One student, Yuki, is feeling nervous."  
Question: "What do you think nervous looks like here?"  
Two illustrated options:  
A: Student fidgeting, tapping pencil, looking around  
B: Student very still, focused, breathing carefully  

Child selects one  

**Step 5: Cultural Expression**  
Story reveals: "Yuki sits very still and breathes carefully. In Yuki's culture, staying calm and composed on the outside is valued‚Äîeven when feeling nervous inside."  
Yuki's thought bubble: "I feel nervous, but I'm managing it by staying focused."  
Teaching: "You learned: Different cultures have different ways of expressing feelings. All are normal and okay!"  
Feeling Guide Book: "Discovery 2: Culture shapes how we show feelings"  

**Step 6: Choice Point**  
Another student, Kenji, finishes the test and bows respectfully to the teacher  
Story: "Kenji feels relieved and happy the test is over. How might Kenji show these feelings?"  
Three choices:  
- Jump up and cheer  
- Bow and smile gently ‚úì (culturally appropriate)  
- Run out of the room  

If child selects culturally appropriate choice, story affirms: "Yes! Kenji shows respect and happiness through a gentle bow and smile. This matches the values in this school."  

---

#### Phase 3: Mexico Story (Minutes 5‚Äì6)

**Step 7: Family Celebration**  
Map zooms to Mexico  
Story: "You're at a big family celebration for Abuela's (grandmother's) birthday. Everyone is feeling happy and excited!"  
Illustration shows large, colorful gathering with music, food, dancing  

**Step 8: Observation**  
Story: "Look around the celebration. You see:"  
- Uncle Carlos: Dancing, singing loudly, hugging everyone  
- Cousin Maria: Talking rapidly, laughing, telling stories  
- Little cousin Luis: Clapping, running around with energy

- All of them ‚úì  
- Only the loud ones  
- Only Abuela  

**Step 9: Teaching**  
Correct answer: "Yes! ALL of them are happy‚Äîbut showing it in their own ways!"  
Abuela's thought bubble: "I feel so happy my family is here. My tears are joy tears!"  
Text: "In this family, feelings are expressed openly and warmly. Happiness can look like dancing, talking, laughing, or even joyful tears!"  
Feeling Guide Book: "Discovery 3: Families have their own expression styles"  

---

#### Phase 4: Kenya Story (Minutes 7‚Äì8)

**Step 10: Community Gathering**  
Map zooms to Kenya  
Story: "You visit a community meeting where people are discussing an important decision. One person, Amara, is feeling worried about the decision."  
Question: "How might Amara express worry here?"  
Two choices:  
- Amara speaks up directly, asking careful questions ‚úì  
- Amara stays completely silent and leaves  

**Step 11: Expression Discovery**  
Story: "Amara speaks thoughtfully, asking questions to understand better. In this community, expressing feelings through respectful discussion is valued."  
Amara's thought: "I feel worried, so I want to make sure we think carefully about this decision."  
Another person, Jabari, listens quietly but nods his head  
Jabari's thought: "I also feel uncertain. I'm thinking deeply about what to do."  
Teaching: "You discovered: Amara shows worry through questions. Jabari shows it through quiet thinking. Both are expressing the same concern!"  
Feeling Guide Book: "Discovery 4: Even the same feeling can look very different"  

---

#### Phase 5: Norway Story (Minutes 9)

**Step 12: Quiet Home Setting**  
Map zooms to Norway  
Story: "You visit a home in Norway. It's a quiet winter evening. The family is together, feeling content and peaceful."  
Illustration shows family reading together, sitting near each other but not talking much  

**Step 13: Subtle Expression**  
Question: "Are they feeling happy and connected?"  
Two choices:  
- No‚Äîthey should be talking and laughing if they're happy  
- Yes‚Äîquiet togetherness can show happiness and connection ‚úì  

**Step 14: Cultural Understanding**  
Story: "Yes! In this family, being quietly together is a way of showing comfort and happiness. They don't need lots of words or noise to feel connected."  
One child's thought: "I feel so peaceful and happy here with my family."  
Teaching: "You learned: Some cultures and families show feelings through quiet presence. It's just as meaningful as loud expression!"  
Feeling Guide Book: "Discovery 5: Quiet can be just as expressive as loud"  

---

#### Phase 6: Reflection & Synthesis (Final 2 minutes)

**Step 15: Return Home**  
Map returns to home location  
Story: "You've traveled around the world! What did you discover about feelings and expressions?"  
Feeling Guide Book opens, showing all 5 discoveries  

**Step 16: Understanding Check**  
Question 1: "Are feelings the same everywhere?"  
- Yes‚Äîall people have feelings ‚úì  
- No‚Äîsome people don't have feelings  

Question 2: "Are expressions the same everywhere?"  
- Yes‚Äîeveryone shows feelings the same way  
- No‚Äîexpressions are shaped by culture, family, and personality ‚úì  

Question 3: "When you see someone expressing differently than you, what should you do?"  
- Assume they're wrong  
- Be curious and respectful ‚úì  
- Tell them to act like you  

**Step 17: Completion**  
Story concludes: "You're now a world traveler who understands: Feelings are universal (everyone has them), but expressions are diverse (everyone shows them their own way). Both things are true!"  
Final message: "Being respectful and curious about different expressions helps us understand each other better."

### Continuity Note
This game brings Lesson 4's core message to life through narrative exploration: while all humans share feelings (Lesson 1's foundation), the ways we express them vary by culture, family, and individual personality. This completes the unit arc, showing feelings as information (L1), embodied experiences (L2), temporary states (L3), and universally human yet uniquely expressed (L4).

## Home Game 4B ‚Äì Feelings Detective Agency
> Jump to: [Setup](#setup-1) ¬∑ [Play](#play-1) ¬∑ [Scoring](#scoring-1) ¬∑ [Win Condition](#win-condition-1) ¬∑ [Continuity Note](#continuity-note-1)

### Learning Objective
Students will demonstrate empathy by acknowledging that different expressions are valid, apply curiosity by asking "What might that person be feeling?" rather than assuming, and solidify understanding that feelings are universal while expressions vary.

### Game File Name & Format
`hg-06.04-02.html` ‚Äî Life Simulation (from Generic: Simulation Games)

### Game Purpose
Children run a "Feelings Detective Agency" where they help characters understand each other's feelings by investigating behaviors, asking questions, and making observations‚Äîrather than jumping to assumptions. This builds practical empathy skills and reinforces that observable behavior (expression) doesn't always reveal internal experience (feeling).

### Estimated Play Time
10‚Äì12 minutes

---

### Setup
**Opening Screen:**  
"Welcome to the Feelings Detective Agency! Your job: help people understand each other's feelings by being curious, not assuming!"  

**Main Display:**  
Detective office with desk, case files, and observation tools  
Character (Detective You) sits at desk  
Phone rings with new cases  

**Case Files:**  
Shows 5 cases to solve  

**Detective Tools:**  
- Magnifying Glass (observe carefully)  
- Question Mark (ask curious questions)  
- Notepad (record discoveries)  
- Heart (show empathy)  

**Visual Style:**  
Friendly detective theme (cartoon, not serious), bright office, supportive tone  

---

### Play

#### Phase 1: Training Case (Minutes 1‚Äì2)

**Step 1: First Case Arrives**  
Phone rings: "We have a case! Please help!"  
Case file appears: "Case 1: The Quiet Kid"  
Situation: "Teacher reports: 'Jamie sits alone at lunch every day. Other kids think Jamie is sad and unfriendly.'"  
Your task: "Find out what Jamie is really feeling!"  

**Step 2: Investigation Options**  
Three detective approaches appear:  
- Option A: "Tell Jamie they should smile more so people know they're happy" ‚ùå  
- Option B: "Observe Jamie carefully, then ask curious questions" ‚úì  
- Option C: "Assume Jamie is sad because they're alone" ‚ùå  
Child selects Option B (if wrong choice, gentle guidance offers retry)  

**Step 3: Observation Phase**  
Magnifying glass activates  
Scene shows Jamie sitting alone with a book, small smile on face  
Observation checklist appears:  
- Body language: Relaxed, comfortable  
- Facial expression: Gentle smile  
- Activity: Reading, engaged  

Detective You notes: "Interesting! Jamie looks peaceful, not upset."  

**Step 4: Asking Questions**  
Question Mark tool activates  
Child selects questions to ask Jamie (can choose multiple):  
- "Are you feeling sad?" ‚úì  
- "Do you like sitting alone?" ‚úì  
- "Why are you being unfriendly?" ‚ùå  

Jamie responds: "I'm not sad! I actually love reading during lunch. It's my quiet happy time. I have friends, but I also like being alone sometimes."  

**Step 5: Case Solved**  
Notepad updates: "Discovery: Jamie is happy‚Äîjust expressing it quietly through reading alone. Quiet doesn't mean sad!"  
Heart tool activates: "Show empathy"  
Message to classmates: "Jamie likes quiet time alone. That's okay! Some people show happiness with friends, others show it with peaceful activities."  
Case File stamped: SOLVED  
Feedback: "You discovered: We can't assume feelings from behavior. We need to observe and ask!"  

---

#### Phase 2: Four More Cases (Minutes 3‚Äì9)

**Case 2: The Laughing Friend**

Discovery: "Same expression (laughing) can mean different feelings (happy OR nervous)!"  

**Case 3: The Angry Artist**

Discovery: "Facial expressions can be misread. What looks angry might just be focus!"  

**Case 4: The Crying Winner**

Case file: "Jordan won the science fair and started crying. Everyone is confused. Are they sad?"  
Observation: Tears, but also smiling  
Questions reveal: "I'm so happy I could cry! These are happy tears. I worked so hard on this project!"  
Discovery: "Crying doesn't always mean sad. People can have happy tears, relief tears, proud tears!"  

**Case 5: The Silent Partner**  
Final Case  
Case file: "During partner work, Casey didn't talk much. The other student thinks Casey didn't care."  
Investigation process (full sequence):  
Observe: Casey was listening carefully, taking notes, working quietly  
Ask questions: "Were you interested in the project?"  
Casey responds: "Yes! I love this project. I'm a quiet worker. I like to think before I speak."  
Discovery: "Quiet participation is still participation! Different people contribute differently."

## How Lesson 4 Fits into the Full Learning Arc

Lesson 4 completes the journey of **Unit 6: What Are Feelings?** by expanding students‚Äô awareness from *self-understanding* to *understanding others*. The two home games‚Äî*Expression Around the World* and *Feelings Detective Agency*‚Äîtranslate empathy into a structured, cognitive skill: noticing, questioning, and interpreting feelings through observation rather than assumption. Together, they illustrate that while feelings are **biologically universal**, expressions are **culturally and personally diverse**.  

This final lesson integrates all prior insights:
- **Lesson 1** taught that feelings are information messengers.  
- **Lesson 2** revealed that feelings appear in the body.  
- **Lesson 3** showed that feelings change over time.  
- **Lesson 4** now demonstrates that everyone has feelings, but the *way* we show them differs across individuals, families, and cultures.  

> üß≠ **Learning Arc Summary:**  
> Inner Awareness ‚Üí Body Awareness ‚Üí Temporal Awareness ‚Üí Social Awareness.  
> By the end of Unit 6, students see emotional life as both *scientifically observable* and *deeply human*: feelings connect us all, yet their expressions form a mosaic of individuality. Understanding this duality builds empathy grounded in curiosity‚Äîan essential bridge to the social systems explored in Unit 7 (‚ÄúFamily and Friendship Dynamics‚Äù).
