## HOME GAME 3A: Conflict Navigator

### Learning Objective

Students will apply team problem-solving strategies (listening to all ideas, sharing feelings respectfully, finding compromises, agreeing on plans) and will distinguish between unproductive conflict patterns and productive solution-finding.

### Game File Name

hg-10.03-01.html

### Game Format

**Interactive Fiction (Adventure Game Format)**

Players navigate a branching story where their choices about conflict resolution lead to different outcomes. This format emphasizes decision-making, consequence awareness, and the application of problem-solving strategies.

### Purpose

Students practice applying conflict resolution strategies in realistic scenarios. They discover through experience that choosing to listen, compromise, and find solutions together leads to positive outcomes, while arguing without listening creates breakdown.

### Estimated Play Time

8‚Äì12 minutes (depending on path taken and replays)

---

### Setup

**Opening Screen:**

- Title: "Conflict Navigator"
- Subtitle: "Your team has a problem. What will you do?"
- Visual: Cartoon avatar (player character) standing at a fork in the road with directional signs
- Start button: "Begin Story"

**Instructions:**

- "You're part of a team working on a project. Problems will happen! When they do, you'll choose how to handle them. Your choices matter‚Äîthey'll change what happens next!"
- Visual guide showing two paths:
    - ‚úÖ "Solving Together" path (green) = Listen, compromise, find solutions
    - ‚ùå "Not Solving" path (red) = Argue, don't listen, give up

---

### Play

**Branching Story Structure (3 Major Conflict Points):**

The story follows a team of four kids (including the player) working on a class project to build a model town.

**OPENING SCENE:**

- "Your teacher says: 'Class, we're building a model town together! Work in teams of four.'"
- Your team: You, Maya, Alex, and Jordan
- "Everyone's excited! But then..."

**CONFLICT 1: Disagreement About the Plan**

**Situation:**

- Text: "Maya says: 'Let's build a town with lots of houses!' Alex says: 'No! I want a town with a big park!' They both look upset."
- Visual: Two avatars facing each other with frustrated expressions

**Choice Point:** What do you do?

**Option A:** "Say: 'Stop arguing! Let's listen to both ideas.'"

- **Leads to:** PRODUCTIVE PATH
- Next scene: "You say: 'Maya, why do you want houses?' Maya: 'Because people need homes!' You say: 'Alex, why a park?' Alex: 'So kids have a place to play!' Jordan: 'What if we build BOTH? Houses around a central park?'"
- Outcome: Everyone smiles. "Great idea! Let's do it!"
- **Continue to Conflict 2 (Productive)**

**Option B:** "Say: 'Maya's idea is better. Let's do houses.'"

- **Leads to:** UNPRODUCTIVE PATH
- Next scene: Alex looks hurt. "That's not fair! No one listened to me!" Alex walks away from the table.
- Jordan looks worried. "Now we only have three people..."
- Outcome: Team weakened
- **Continue to Conflict 2 (Unproductive)**

**Option C:** "Stay quiet and hope they figure it out."

- **Leads to:** UNPRODUCTIVE PATH
- Next scene: Maya and Alex keep arguing. Time passes. Nothing gets built. Jordan says: "We're not getting anywhere..."
- Outcome: No progress made
- **Continue to Conflict 2 (Unproductive)**

**CONFLICT 2A: Materials Problem (PRODUCTIVE PATH)**

**Situation:**

- Text: "Your team is building the town with houses AND a park. But then... Jordan says: 'We only have enough green paper for the park OR the trees. Not both. What should we do?'"
- Visual: Table with limited materials

**Choice Point:** What do you do?

**Option A:** "Say: 'Let's vote! Everyone share your idea first.'"

- Next scene: "Maya: 'I think park.' Alex: 'I think trees.' Jordan: 'I think park.' You vote for trees. The team votes: 3 for park, 1 for trees."
- You say: "Okay, I wanted trees, but I can live with that. The team decided together!"
- Outcome: Everyone feels respected. Park gets built. Team continues strong.
- **Continue to Conflict 3 (Strong Team)**

**Option B:** "Say: 'What if we use different materials? Can we make trees with brown and green crayons instead?'"

- Next scene: "Jordan: 'Great creative thinking!' The team draws trees with crayons and uses green paper for the park."
- Maya: "Problem solved!"
- Outcome: Creative compromise found. Team feels proud.
- **Continue to Conflict 3 (Strong Team)**

**CONFLICT 2B: Materials Problem (UNPRODUCTIVE PATH)**

**Situation:**

- Text: "Your team is struggling. Alex left earlier, so now it's just you, Maya, and Jordan. Jordan says: 'We need more glue, but it's all gone. What do we do?'"

**Choice Point:** What do you do?

**Option A:** "Say: 'This is too hard. Let's give up.'"

- Next scene: Team puts materials away. Project incomplete. Teacher asks: "What happened?" Team looks down, embarrassed.
- Outcome: GAME OVER - Restart from Conflict 1
- Message: "When teams give up instead of solving problems together, projects don't get finished. Try again?"

**Option B:** "Say: 'Wait! Let's ask for help and find a solution together.'"

- Next scene: "You go to the teacher: 'We need glue.' Teacher: 'Here's more!' You return to the team: 'Let's also ask Alex to come back. We need everyone.'"
- Maya: "Good idea! Alex, we're sorry we didn't listen. Come back?"
- Alex: "Okay! Let's work together this time."
- Outcome: Team rebuilds! **Path redirects to Conflict 3 (Recovered Team)**

**CONFLICT 3A: Final Challenge (STRONG TEAM PATH)**

**Situation:**

- Text: "Your town is almost done! But there's one problem: it's time to present, and everyone wants to talk. Maya says: 'I'll present!' Alex says: 'No, I will!' Jordan says: 'Me too!'"

**Choice Point:** What do you do?

**Option A:** "Say: 'Everyone should have a turn! Let's each present one part.'"

- Next scene: "The team agrees! Maya presents the houses. Alex presents the park. Jordan presents the trees. You present the roads."
- Teacher: "What excellent teamwork! You all contributed!"
- Class applauds.
- Outcome: **SUCCESS ENDING**
- Message: "Your team solved problems together by listening, compromising, and making fair decisions. You built trust and finished strong!"

**Option B:** "Say: 'Let's have the person who worked hardest present everything.'"

- Next scene: Team looks confused. "We all worked hard..." Someone feels left out.
- Presentation happens, but not everyone feels valued.
- Outcome: **OKAY ENDING**
- Message: "Your project got done, but not everyone felt included. Remember: strong teams make sure everyone's contribution matters!"

**CONFLICT 3B: Final Challenge (RECOVERED TEAM PATH)**

**Situation:**

- Text: "Your team worked through problems and came back together. Now it's time to present! But you're running out of time. What should you do?"

**Choice Point:** What do you do?

**Option A:** "Say: 'Let's work together really fast! Everyone take one section!'"

- Next scene: Team splits tasks. Maya finishes houses quickly, Alex adds park details, Jordan adds finishing touches, you organize everything.
- Presentation happens. Not perfect, but complete.
- Teacher: "You worked through challenges and didn't give up. That's real teamwork!"
- Outcome: **GROWTH ENDING**
- Message: "Your team faced problems, but you solved them by working together. You discovered that teams get stronger when they solve problems cooperatively!"

**SUMMARY SCREEN (After Any Ending):**

- Display path taken (visual map showing choices made)
- Key Discovery Statement based on ending reached
- Reflection questions:
    - "What problem-solving strategies did you use?"
    - "How did listening help your team?"
    - "What would you do differently next time?"
- "Play Again" button (try different choices)

---

### Scoring

**Story Completion Badges:**

- Reach SUCCESS ending: "Master Problem Solver" badge (gold)
- Reach GROWTH ending: "Team Builder" badge (silver)
- Reach OKAY ending: "Getting There" badge (bronze)
- Try all three endings: "Story Explorer" bonus badge

**Strategy Points:**

- Each time player chooses listening/compromise: +2 points
- Each time player chooses arguing/avoiding: 0 points
- Creative problem-solving: +1 bonus point

**Total possible:** 15 points (by exploring multiple paths and making productive choices)

---

### Win Condition

Player reaches any ending (SUCCESS, GROWTH, or OKAY). Multiple completions encouraged to explore different problem-solving paths. Win message varies by ending achieved.

## HOME GAME 3B: Pattern Detective

### Learning Objective

Students will distinguish between unproductive conflict patterns (arguing without listening) and productive ones (cooperative solution-finding), and will recognize that solving problems together builds team trust and resilience.

### Game File Name

hg-10.03-02.html

### Game Format

**Observation Challenge (Science App Format)**

Players watch team conflict scenarios and identify whether the pattern is productive or unproductive. This format builds pattern recognition and analytical observation skills.

### Purpose

Students develop the ability to recognize conflict patterns as they unfold and distinguish between destructive and constructive team behaviors. They learn to identify early warning signs of team breakdown versus signs of healthy problem-solving.

### Estimated Play Time

10‚Äì12 minutes

---

### Setup

**Opening Screen:**

- Title: "Pattern Detective"
- Subtitle: "Can you spot the pattern? Productive or Unproductive?"
- Visual: Detective badge and magnifying glass over team scenarios
- Start button: "Start Investigating"

**Instructions:**

- "You're a Pattern Detective! You'll watch teams facing problems. Your job: Figure out if they're solving problems the PRODUCTIVE way or the UNPRODUCTIVE way."
- Visual guide showing:
    - üü¢ PRODUCTIVE PATTERN = Listening, compromising, finding solutions together
    - üî¥ UNPRODUCTIVE PATTERN = Arguing without listening, giving up, excluding others

---

### Play

**8 Video Scenarios (15‚Äì20 seconds each):**

Each scenario shows a brief animated conflict. After watching, player identifies the pattern.

**SCENARIO 1: The Puzzle Problem**

**Video:**

- Two kids working on a puzzle
- Kid 1: "This piece goes here!"
- Kid 2: "No! It goes there!"
- Kid 1: "You never listen to me!" (raises voice)
- Kid 2: "Well YOU never listen to ME!" (raises voice)
- Both kids push puzzle away. Storm off in different directions.
- Puzzle remains unfinished.

**Question:** "What pattern did you observe?"

**Choices:**

- A) Productive ‚Äì they're solving the problem together
- B) Unproductive ‚Äì they're arguing without listening

**Correct:** B

**Feedback:** "Correct! This is an UNPRODUCTIVE pattern. They argued without listening, and no one solved the problem. The puzzle stayed unfinished, and both kids felt upset."

**Detective Note Added:** "üî¥ Pattern spotted: Arguing without listening ‚Üí Problem stays unsolved"

**SCENARIO 2: The Soccer Game Decision**

**Video:**

- Three kids choosing which game to play
- Kid 1: "I want to play soccer!"
- Kid 2: "I hear you. I want to play tag."
- Kid 3: "I like both! What if we vote?"
- All three kids: "Good idea!"
- They vote. Two pick soccer, one picks tag.
- Kid 2 (who voted for tag): "Okay! Soccer it is. Let's play!"
- All three run off happily together.

**Question:** "What pattern did you observe?"

**Choices:**

- A) Productive ‚Äì they listened and found a solution together
- B) Unproductive ‚Äì they gave up

**Correct:** A

**Feedback:** "Yes! This is a PRODUCTIVE pattern. They listened to each other's ideas, compromised by voting, and everyone respected the decision. The team stayed together!"

**Detective Note Added:** "üü¢ Pattern spotted: Listening + compromise ‚Üí Problem solved, team strong"

**SCENARIO 3: The Art Supplies Conflict**

**Video:**

- Two kids both reach for the last blue marker
- Kid 1: "I need it!"
- Kid 2: "But I had it first!"
- Kid 1: "FINE! Take it! I don't care!" (storms away angrily)
- Kid 2 stands alone with marker, looking sad
- Neither kid continues their art project

**Question:** "What pattern did you observe?"

**Choices:**

- A) Productive ‚Äì one person got what they wanted
- B) Unproductive ‚Äì one person gave up without solving the problem

**Correct:** B

**Feedback:** "Right! This is UNPRODUCTIVE. Even though Kid 2 got the marker, no one solved the problem fairly. Kid 1 gave up without trying to find a solution together. Both kids ended up feeling bad."

**Detective Note Added:** "üî¥ Pattern spotted: Giving up without solving ‚Üí Nobody wins"

**SCENARIO 4: The Building Challenge**

**Video:**

- Four kids building with blocks
- Kid 1's tower falls over

- Kid 2: "That's okay! Let me help you rebuild it."
- Kid 3: "I have an idea‚Äîlet's make the base wider so it's stronger!"

- All kids cheer

**Question:** "What pattern did you observe?"

**Choices:**

- A) Productive ‚Äì they turned a problem into a team solution
- B) Unproductive ‚Äì one person fixed it alone

**Correct:** A

**Feedback:** "Excellent detective work! This is PRODUCTIVE. When a problem happened, the team supported each other, shared ideas, and worked together to make things even better!"

**Detective Note Added:** "üü¢ Pattern spotted: Problem + teamwork ‚Üí Even better outcome"

**SCENARIO 5: The Snack Share Argument**

**Video:**

- Three kids with one cookie to share

- Kid 2: "No! It's mine!"
- Kid 3: "Stop fighting! What if we break it into three pieces?"

**Choices:**

- A) Productive ‚Äì they tried to find a solution

**Detective Note Added:** "üî¥ Pattern spotted: Ignoring solutions ‚Üí Everyone loses"

**SCENARIO 6: The Group Project Roles**

## How Lesson 3 Fits into the Full Learning Arc

Lesson 3 moves the unit from understanding _how_ teams work (roles and listening) into understanding _what to do when teamwork breaks down_. Through Home Game 3A (Conflict Navigator), students actively make choices inside branching conflict scenarios, practising strategies such as listening, sharing feelings respectfully, finding compromises, and repairing relationships. Home Game 3B (Pattern Detective) then helps them step back and recognise the underlying patterns behind conflict‚Äîspotting the difference between unproductive behaviours (arguing without listening, blaming, giving up) and productive ones (cooperating, compromising, building solutions together). Together, these games transform ‚Äúconflict‚Äù from something scary or personal into something predictable, observable, and solvable.

- **Lesson 1:** Introduced the foundation of teamwork‚Äîteams achieve more than individuals, and cooperation is distinct from simply being together. Students experienced how shared goals transform what a group can accomplish.
- **Lesson 2:** Explained the internal mechanics of teamwork. Students learned that roles (Leader, Organizer, Creator, Supporter) and listening skills allow teams to coordinate smoothly. They practised adjusting ‚Äúlistening level‚Äù and saw directly how it affects team outcomes.
- **Lesson 3 (this document):** Extends this understanding into problem-solving. Students apply strategies that repair breakdowns‚Äîlistening to all ideas, sharing feelings respectfully, compromising, and making fair decisions. They also learn to identify conflict patterns, giving them the analytical tools to predict and prevent team failure.
- **Lesson 4:** Completes the unit by shifting focus from conflict to cohesion. Students learn to notice contributions, celebrate achievements, and reinforce team trust‚Äîcementing the idea that strong teams recognise and value everyone‚Äôs role in the final success.

üîß **Learning Arc Summary:** Lesson 1 defines teamwork; Lesson 2 explains how it works; Lesson 3 teaches how to fix it when things go wrong; Lesson 4 shows how to strengthen it through shared recognition.  
By the end of Unit 10, students see that problems are normal in teamwork‚Äîand that they have the skills and strategies to turn conflicts into cooperation, preventing breakdown and building stronger, more resilient teams.
