## HOME GAME 4A: Team Trophy Builder

### Learning Objective

Students will explain that celebrating collective achievements strengthens team spirit and belonging, and will distinguish between celebrating "winning" versus celebrating teamwork, effort, and growth.

### Game File Name

hg-10.04-01.html

### Game Format

**Drag-and-Drop Lab (Science App Format)**

Players combine different celebration elements to create team trophies, discovering which types of recognition strengthen team bonds. This format teaches cause-effect relationships in a hands-on, experimental way.

### Purpose

Students experiment with different types of celebration and recognition to discover that collective celebration (focusing on teamwork and shared effort) builds stronger team connections than individual recognition (focusing on winning or being best).

### Estimated Play Time

8‚Äì10 minutes

---

### Setup

**Opening Screen:**

- Title: "Team Trophy Builder"
- Subtitle: "What kind of celebration makes teams strongest?"
- Visual: A trophy workshop with shelves of celebration elements
- Start button: "Build Trophies"

**Instructions:**

- "You'll build trophies for teams! Each trophy celebrates something different. After you build it, you'll see how it affects the team. Which celebration makes the team feel best?"
- Visual guide showing workshop with draggable elements

---

### Play

**Trophy Workshop Interface:**

Players see a central trophy platform and shelves with draggable celebration elements organized into categories.

**Available Elements (Draggable Items):**

**Shelf 1: WHO Gets Recognized**

- "One Star Player" badge
- "Whole Team Together" badge
- "The Winner" badge
- "Everyone Who Helped" badge

**Shelf 2: WHAT Gets Celebrated**

- "Being the Best" ribbon
- "Working Together" ribbon
- "Winning the Game" ribbon
- "Trying Hard & Growing" ribbon

**Shelf 3: HOW We Celebrate**

- "One Person Takes a Bow" card
- "Everyone Takes a Bow Together" card
- "Trophy for First Place" card
- "Thank You to Each Person" card

**EXPERIMENT 1: The Soccer Team**

**Scenario Setup:**

- Text: "A soccer team just finished a game. They scored 3 goals and worked really well together. Build a trophy for them!"
- Visual: Cartoon soccer team of 5 kids standing together

**Player Task:** Drag 3 elements (one from each shelf) onto the trophy platform

**Experiment A (Player drags):**

- "One Star Player" + "Being the Best" + "One Person Takes a Bow"
- **Result Animation:**
    - One kid steps forward, receives trophy, smiles
    - Other 4 kids look down, disappointed
    - Team splits apart, walks away separately
- **Outcome Data:** "Team Connection Meter: ‚¨áÔ∏è LOW (2/10)"
- **Observation:** "When only one person gets celebrated, others feel left out. The team doesn't feel connected."

**Experiment B (Player rebuilds):**

- "Whole Team Together" + "Working Together" + "Everyone Takes a Bow Together"
- **Result Animation:**
    - All 5 kids step forward together
    - Trophy shows all their names
    - Kids high-five, hug, cheer together
    - Team stays close, celebrates as a group
- **Outcome Data:** "Team Connection Meter: ‚¨ÜÔ∏è HIGH (9/10)"
- **Observation:** "When the whole team is celebrated for working together, everyone feels valued! The team bond is strong."

**EXPERIMENT 2: The Class Project Team**

**Scenario Setup:**

- Text: "Four kids just finished a big class project. They had some disagreements, but they worked through them and completed it together. Build their trophy!"
- Visual: Four kids with completed project (poster)

**Player Task:** Drag 3 elements to build trophy

- "The Winner" + "Being the Best" + "Trophy for First Place"

**Experiment B:**

- **Result:**
    - Trophy lists what each kid contributed: "Maya: Great ideas! Alex: Organized materials! Jordan: Beautiful art! Sam: Encouraging words!"
    - All kids smile, point to their names, feel proud
    - Team hugs, says "We did this together!"
    - Team Connection Meter: ‚¨ÜÔ∏è HIGH (10/10)
- **Observation:** "When we celebrate each person's contribution AND the team's effort, everyone feels important and proud!"

**EXPERIMENT 3: The Cleanup Crew**

**Scenario Setup:**

- Text: "Three kids volunteered to clean up after art time. It was hard work, but they got it done! Build their trophy!"
- Visual: Three kids with broom, sponge, organizing supplies

**Player Task:** Build trophy

**Experiment B:**

- **Result:**
    - Trophy says: "Thank you to our Cleanup Team! You all worked hard together!"
    - All three kids smile: "We did it as a team!"
    - Team high-fives, feels proud together
    - Team Connection Meter: ‚¨ÜÔ∏è HIGH (9/10)

# HOME GAME 4B: Our Team Story

## Learning Objective

Students will demonstrate appreciation for team members' contributions by acknowledging specific helpful actions, and will recognize that cooperation itself is rewarding and meaningful, making future teamwork something to anticipate positively.

## Game File Name

hg-10.04-02.html

## Game Format

**Point-and-Click Adventure (Adventure Game Format)**

Players create a team story by clicking on characters and scenes to highlight team contributions, building a celebration narrative. This format emphasizes recognition, appreciation, and reflection on the teamwork journey.

## Purpose

Students practice identifying and appreciating specific contributions team members make, creating a celebration story that honors everyone's role. They discover that recognizing cooperation builds motivation for future teamwork.

## Estimated Play Time

**10‚Äì12 minutes**

---

## Setup

**Opening Screen:**

- Title: "Our Team Story"
- Subtitle: "Create a celebration story about your team!"
- Visual: A storybook with blank pages and four cartoon avatars (customizable to represent the player and three teammates)
- Start button: "Create Our Story"

**Instructions:**

- "You just finished a big team project! Now it's time to celebrate by creating a team story. Click on teammates to discover what they did, then add their contributions to your celebration story!"
- Visual: Interactive storybook interface

---

## Play

### STORY CREATION PROCESS

The game presents a team of four kids (player + 3 teammates: Maya, Alex, Jordan) who just completed a project together. Players build the celebration story across 5 story pages.

### PAGE 1: Our Team Formed

**Scene:** Four avatars standing together at the beginning of a project

**Interactive Elements:**

- Click on Avatar 1 (Maya): Speech bubble appears: "I was excited! I wanted to help!"
- Click on Avatar 2 (Alex): "Me too! I like working with friends!"
- Click on Avatar 3 (Jordan): "I wasn't sure what to do at first, but I was ready to try!"
- Click on You (Player): "I was ready to be part of the team!"

**Story Prompt:** "How should we start our celebration story?"

**Options (player selects one):**

- A) "We formed a team because we wanted to work together!"
- B) "We were put in a team and had to work together."
- C) "We became a team and everyone was excited to cooperate!"

**Recommended choice:** A or C (emphasizes voluntary cooperation and positive attitude)

**Story Page 1 Result:**

- Selected text appears in storybook with illustration of team forming
- Caption: "Our team story begins!"

### PAGE 2: Everyone Had a Role

**Scene:** Team working on project, each person doing something different

**Interactive Elements:**

- Click on Maya: "I had the idea for our project! I was the Creator!"
- Click on Alex: "I organized all our materials and kept us on track! I was the Organizer!"
- Click on Jordan: "I encouraged everyone when we were tired! I was the Supporter!"
- Click on You: "I helped lead and make decisions! I was the Leader!"

**Story Prompt:** "What should we say about our roles?"

**Options:**

- A) "The leader was the most important person."
- B) "Everyone had an important role, and we all needed each other!"
- C) "Some roles were more important than others."

**Correct choice:** B (collective recognition)

**Story Page 2 Result:**

- Text: "Everyone had an important role, and we all needed each other!"
- Illustration: All four avatars with their role labels, standing proudly
- Interactive feature: Click each avatar to see their contribution highlighted with sparkles

### PAGE 3: We Faced a Challenge

**Scene:** Team facing a problem (building materials scattered, confused expressions)

**Interactive Elements:**

- Click on problem area: "Oh no! We ran out of glue and didn't agree on colors!"
- Click on Maya: "I felt frustrated. But I knew we could solve it!"
- Click on Alex: "I was worried. But then I remembered we're a team!"
- Click on Jordan: "I felt sad. But I wanted to help fix it!"
- Click on You: "I felt stressed. But I knew we could work through it together!"

**Story Prompt:** "How did our team handle the challenge?"

**Options:**

- A) "One person solved the problem for everyone."
- B) "We listened to each other and found a solution together!"
- C) "We gave up because it was too hard."

**Correct choice:** B

**Story Page 3 Result:**

- Text: "We listened to each other and found a solution together!"
- Illustration: Team gathered in a circle, talking and problem-solving
- Speech bubbles appear showing: "Let's ask the teacher for glue!" "Let's vote on colors!" "Great ideas!"

### PAGE 4: We Succeeded Together

**Scene:** Completed project (beautiful poster/model/creation) with team admiring it

**Interactive Elements:**

- Click on project: Zoom in on details, with labels showing who contributed what
    - "Maya's creative idea" ‚Üí points to main concept
    - "Alex's organized layout" ‚Üí points to neat arrangement
    - "Jordan's encouraging words" ‚Üí points to team spirit notes
    - "Your leadership" ‚Üí points to coordinated execution
- Click on each avatar: They express pride
    - Maya: "I'm proud of what WE made!"
    - Alex: "This is OUR achievement!"
    - Jordan: "We did this TOGETHER!"
    - You: "Every person was important!"

**Story Prompt:** "What made our team successful?"

**Options:**

- A) "We were the best team and won."
- B) "Everyone contributed, listened, and worked together toward our shared goal!"
- C) "We worked really fast."

**Correct choice:** B

**Story Page 4 Result:**

- Text: "Everyone contributed, listened, and worked together toward our shared goal!"
- Illustration: Completed project with all four avatars around it, hands touching the project
- Interactive: Click project to see "team effort" sparkles animate across all contributions

### PAGE 5: Our Celebration

**Scene:** Team celebration moment - all avatars together, happy expressions

**Interactive Elements:**

- Click on celebration banner: "Celebrating Our Teamwork!"
- Click on each avatar to add their celebration message:
    - Maya: "Thank you, Alex, for keeping us organized!"
    - Alex: "Thank you, Jordan, for encouraging us!"
    - Jordan: "Thank you, \[Player\], for leading us!"
    - You (player chooses):
        - "Thank you, Maya, for your creative ideas!"
        - "Thank you, everyone, for being an amazing team!"
        - "Thank you all for your different contributions!"

**Story Prompt:** "What should we remember about teamwork?"

**Options:**

- A) "Teamwork is hard and not always fun."
- B) "Teamwork is rewarding when we cooperate, and I want to work in teams again!"
- C) "Teamwork means someone has to be in charge."

**Correct choice:** B

**Story Page 5 Result:**

- Text: "Teamwork is rewarding when we cooperate, and I want to work in teams again!"
- Illustration: All four avatars celebrating together, with hearts and stars around them
- Final message: "We celebrated our teamwork, and now we're ready for the next adventure together!"

## How Lesson 4 Fits into the Full Learning Arc

Lesson 4 completes the teamwork arc by shifting the focus from problem-solving to appreciation and collective pride. Through Home Game 4A (Team Trophy Builder), students experiment with how different types of celebration‚Äîindividual recognition, winning-based praise, or collective appreciation‚Äîaffect team connection. Through Home Game 4B (Our Team Story), they build a personal celebration narrative that highlights each teammate‚Äôs contributions and the journey they took together. These experiences show that strong teams do not only _work_ together; they also _recognise_ each other, reinforcing a sense of belonging that motivates future cooperation.

- **Lesson 1:** Introduced the core idea that teams achieve more than individuals and that teamwork is defined by shared effort toward a common goal. Students distinguished real cooperation from merely being side-by-side.
- **Lesson 2:** Explained the internal mechanisms that make teamwork function‚Äîrole distribution and listening. Students discovered how coordinating roles and attending to others leads to smooth collaboration.
- **Lesson 3:** Showed how teams repair breakdowns. Students practised solving problems together, using strategies like listening, compromise, and fair decision-making, and learned to recognise productive versus unproductive conflict patterns.
- **Lesson 4 (this document):** Brings closure to the unit by emphasising shared recognition. Students discover that celebrating everyone‚Äôs contribution reinforces motivation, trust, and team identity‚Äîmaking teamwork something they want to join again in the future.

üåü **Learning Arc Summary:** Lesson 1 defines teamwork; Lesson 2 shows how it works; Lesson 3 shows how to fix it; Lesson 4 shows how to strengthen it through shared celebration.  
By the end of Unit 10, students understand teamwork not only as coordinated effort but as a relationship built on listening, solving problems together, and appreciating each person's contribution‚Äîlaying the foundation for resilient, future-oriented cooperation.
