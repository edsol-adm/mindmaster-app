This document summarises **teacher observations and professional judgements** gathered during Prototype Alpha v3 testing.

Although testing sessions involved **Grade 3 students**, teachers were asked to assess the **developmental appropriateness of the curriculum content, pedagogy, and interaction design** for **younger learners**, particularly **Grade 1**, which is the primary target group for PowerMind.

Observations are synthesised and anonymised. No individual teacher identifiers are included.

---

## 1. Overall Pedagogical Fit

Teachers consistently reported that:
- The core psychology concepts were **age-appropriate and clearly framed**
- Learning objectives were concrete and understandable
- Activities aligned well with how early primary learners engage with social and emotional concepts

Teachers noted that Grade 3 students completed tasks comfortably, which reinforced their judgement that **the cognitive demands were well within reach of Grade 1 learners** when introduced with appropriate pacing.

**Implication:**  
The curriculum content and pedagogical framing are suitable for early primary grades, including Grade 1.

---

## 2. Language Level and Instructional Design

Teachers observed that:
- Language used in instructions was simple, direct, and concrete
- Vocabulary demands were low and well-supported by visuals
- Instructions could be followed without additional explanation

Teachers indicated that the **clarity and simplicity of language** would be especially beneficial for Grade 1 students, who are still developing reading confidence.

**Implication:**  
Instructional language supports accessibility for younger learners and reduces reliance on adult mediation.

---

## 3. Pacing and Classroom Use

Teachers reported that:
- Activity length and pacing were appropriate for structured classroom sessions
- Tasks could be integrated into lessons without disrupting flow
- The prototype supported focused engagement without requiring constant teacher intervention

Teachers noted that pacing could be **slightly adjusted or segmented** for Grade 1 use, but that the overall structure was appropriate.

**Implication:**  
The activity design supports flexible classroom integration across early primary grades.

---

## 4. Student Autonomy and Support Needs

Teachers observed that:
- Students were able to work independently for most tasks
- Adult intervention was minimal and primarily limited to initial orientation
- Feedback mechanisms supported self-correction

Teachers emphasised that **reducing dependency on adult explanation** is particularly important in Grade 1 classrooms, and viewed this as a strength of the design.

**Implication:**  
The prototype supports early learner autonomy while remaining teacher-friendly.

---

## 5. Visual and Interaction Design

Teachers reported that:
- Visual elements effectively supported comprehension
- Interaction patterns were intuitive and repeatable
- Tasks did not require advanced motor or technical skills

Teachers felt that the **visual-first approach** would be especially effective for Grade 1 learners with varying literacy levels.

**Implication:**  
Interaction and visual design choices support inclusivity and early-grade accessibility.

---

## 6. Developmental Appropriateness for Grade 1

Based on observation and professional judgement, teachers indicated that:
- Content complexity is appropriate for Grade 1 learners
- Concepts can be introduced earlier than Grade 3 without loss of meaning
- Minor adjustments to pacing or scaffolding would be sufficient for Grade 1 classrooms

Teachers viewed testing with Grade 3 students as a **conservative validation approach**, confirming that the material does not exceed early primary cognitive demands.

**Implication:**  
The curriculum is suitable for introduction at Grade 1, with planned refinements to delivery rather than content.

---

## 7. Observed Limitations and Considerations

Teachers noted that:
- Some interactions would benefit from smoother technical performance
- Clearer transition cues could further support younger learners
- Teacher-facing visibility and control would be important in future iterations

These considerations were viewed as **implementation and system-level issues**, not pedagogical concerns.

---

## 8. Summary of Teacher-Level Insights

Teachers concluded that Prototype Alpha v3 demonstrates:
- Strong pedagogical alignment with early primary learning needs
- Appropriate content and cognitive demand for Grade 1 learners
- High potential for classroom integration with minimal teacher burden

These observations support progression beyond Alpha into subsequent development phases focused on **technical refinement and system integration**, rather than curriculum redesign.
